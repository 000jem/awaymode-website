How it works

Three taps from a tab open to work that lands.

The point of Holt is that you stop thinking about it. The session is short to set up, hard to escape from, and silent the rest of the time.

01

Pick what to block.

iOS Screen Time lets you select apps, whole categories (Social, Games, News), or specific websites. Each Holt mode keeps its own list.

02

Choose your length.

Quick is ten minutes. Pomodoro chains four cycles. Deep is ninety minutes of strict, no-bail focus. Or roll your own.

03

Sit with the work.

Tap the timer or tap Fen. The shield goes up. iOS quietly returns a system block if you reach for a flagged app. The timer ends, the apps come back, your sit is logged.

What's under the hood

An iOS app, made the iOS way.

Holt is built entirely on Apple frameworks. No React Native, no web wrapper, no third-party SDK doing surveillance in the background. The native stack is the product.

FAMILY CONTROLS

Real blocking, at the OS level.

Holt uses Apple's FamilyControls and ManagedSettings frameworks to apply a shield at the system level. The apps you select cannot be opened until the session ends. This is the same mechanism Apple's own Screen Time uses.

ACTIVITYKIT

Lock Screen + Dynamic Island.

Live Activities show the remaining time on your Lock Screen and inside the Dynamic Island, with the right tap target to end the session if your mode allows it.

HEALTHKIT

Mindful Minutes, if you ask for them.

Opt in once and every completed focus session writes to Apple Health as Mindful Minutes. Holt only writes; it never reads. Revoke any time in iOS Settings.

SWIFTDATA

History lives only on your phone.

Session logs, mode configurations, and Screen Time selection tokens are stored locally in Apple's SwiftData. There is no cloud sync, no server-side backup, and no analytics SDK reading your data on the side.

APP INTENTS

Siri and Shortcuts ready.

Start a session by voice or via a Shortcut. "Hey Siri, start a Holt session" works from a locked screen. Build a morning automation that opens with a 25-minute sit.

WIDGETKIT

Widgets that mean something.

Home Screen and Lock Screen widgets show your current session or, when idle, today's focused minutes. Updated on every session transition, not on a polling timer.

Modes

Three commitments, plus a fourth you build.

Each mode keeps its own selection of blocked apps. Tap a tile to load it, tap the timer to start it.

Quick
10 min

One block. For the inbox sweep, the hard email, the single design pass before lunch.

Pomodoro
25 × 4 · 5m rest

The classic Cirillo chain. 115 minutes wall-clock with three breaks. Notifications fire at every phase boundary so you know when to step away and when to come back.

Deep
90 min · strict

Strict mode. The shield holds for the entire ninety minutes; the End button is disabled. For the day the work has to land.

Custom
your call

Set the minutes, the cycles, the break length, the blocked apps, and the strictness. Save as many as you need.

What it doesn't do

A short list, on purpose.

Worth knowing before you download.

No iCloud sync (yet).

Holt v1 is single-device on purpose. Less data leaves your phone when there are fewer places for it to go. iCloud sync is on the v1.1 roadmap if there's demand.

No iPad or Mac (yet).

iPhone only at launch. iPad is a small port; Mac is further out. Tell us which you want.

No AI coach.

No suggestions about what you should be focusing on, no nudges, no "summary of your week" emails. The mode is the mode; the work is yours.

No data export (yet).

Your history is yours, but v1 doesn't have a CSV button. If you need to move data out of Holt, email and we'll prioritise it.
