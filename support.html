Getting started

First-launch flow and how a session works.

How do I start my first focus session?
  1. Tap a mode tile at the bottom of the screen (Quick, Pomodoro, or Deep).
  2. Tap the timer in the centre, or tap Fen on the right.
  3. The session begins. The blocked apps disappear for the duration.

If this is your first session and your selected mode has app blocking configured, iOS will ask for Screen Time permission. Approve to start; decline to run the session as a timer only.

What's the difference between tapping Fen and tapping the timer?

Nothing — they both start the same session. Two tap targets are provided because users instinctively reach for the timer or the mascot, and either should work.

Where do notifications come from?

Locally, from UNUserNotificationCenter. Holt schedules them at the moment the session starts, so they fire even if the app is backgrounded or force-quit. For chained modes (Pomodoro), every phase transition gets its own notification: "Break time" at each work-end, "Back to it" at each break-end, and "Done" at the chain's end.

App blocking

Screen Time integration, strict mode, and selection.

Why does Holt need Screen Time access?

Holt uses Apple's FamilyControls framework — the same one that powers Screen Time — to shield the apps you select. Without that permission Holt can still run a timer, but it cannot block anything. The framework keeps the actual app names opaque to Holt; we only see anonymised tokens that work on your device.

How do I select which apps to block?
  1. Open Settings → Modes in Holt.
  2. Tap the mode you want to configure.
  3. Tap Block apps.
  4. Pick apps, categories, or websites in iOS's Screen Time picker. Tap Done.

Each mode keeps its own selection, so Pomodoro can block social media while Deep blocks everything except your editor and design tools.

What does Deep mode do that the other modes don't?

Deep mode is strict. Once you start a session in it, the End button is disabled and the shield holds until the timer runs out. Other modes allow you to end the session early via a confirmation dialog. Use Deep when you genuinely need to remove the option to bail.

Can I bypass the shield in strict mode?

Not from inside Holt. The shield is enforced by iOS at the OS level. Theoretically you could uninstall the app, but iOS's Screen Time will continue to apply restrictions for a brief period after uninstall, and your session history is permanently lost. In practice: don't start a strict session unless you mean it.

Modes

What are the default modes?
  • Quick — one 10-minute block, no chain.
  • Pomodoro — 25-minute work blocks, four cycles, 5-minute breaks in between. 115 minutes wall-clock total.
  • Deep — one 90-minute strict block.
How do I create a custom mode?
  1. On the Timer screen, scroll the modes row to the end and tap + New.
  2. Or open Settings → Modes → tap the + in the section header.
  3. Set the name, duration, cycles, break length, icon, strict toggle, and app selection. Tap Save.

Custom modes appear in the row alongside the built-ins and can be reordered, edited, or deleted from Settings → Modes.

Pomodoro: why does the timer show 25 minutes when the full chain is 115?

The on-screen countdown shows the current work block, not the entire chain. The chain caption directly below the countdown ("4 cycles · 5m rest · 115m total") shows the full commitment. Notifications fire at every phase boundary so you know when to break and when to come back.

History & data

Where does my session history live?

In Apple's SwiftData store, on your device. Holt has no server. There is no cloud copy. Uninstalling Holt removes all session history and configuration permanently.

Can I sync my data across devices?

Not in v1. iCloud sync is on the roadmap. The v1 design intentionally keeps Holt single-device to minimise the data-handling surface area.

How do I export my history?

Export is not in v1. If you need to migrate your history off Holt, email hello@holt.app — we'll prioritise the feature based on demand.

How do I delete my data?

Delete Holt from your iPhone. All session records, custom modes, Screen Time selections, and preferences are removed with the app. There is no Holt server-side copy.

Does Holt write to Apple Health?

Only if you opt in. The toggle is under Settings → Health. When enabled, Holt writes completed sessions to Apple Health as Mindful Minutes. It is a one-way write — Holt never reads from Health. Revoke any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Holt.

Billing

What's included in the free tier?

The Quick and Pomodoro modes are free, and the timer functionality works without payment. App blocking via Screen Time and the Deep strict mode require an unlock. The paywall appears the first time you attempt a paid feature.

How are subscriptions billed?

Through your Apple ID. Monthly and annual subscriptions auto-renew unless cancelled at least 24 hours before the period ends. The annual plan includes a 7-day free trial. Lifetime is a one-time purchase with no renewal.

How do I cancel?
  1. iOS Settings → tap your name at the top.
  2. Subscriptions → Holt → Cancel Subscription.

Holt does not see or manage your subscription state directly; Apple handles all of it.

I bought lifetime — will it transfer to a new iPhone?

Yes. On the new device, open the paywall and tap Restore Purchases. StoreKit will detect your prior purchase under the same Apple ID and unlock the app.

How do I request a refund?

Apple manages all refunds. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com, locate the Holt charge, and request a refund. We have no access to payment information and cannot process refunds from our side.

Troubleshooting

The shield isn't blocking the app I selected.
  1. Confirm Screen Time permission is granted: iOS Settings → Holt → Screen Time should show Allow Family Controls on.
  2. End the current session and start a new one — the shield is applied at session start.
  3. Toggle the iOS Screen Time master setting off and back on (Settings → Screen Time).
  4. Restart the iPhone. Screen Time occasionally wedges; a restart clears it.

If the issue persists, email us with your iOS version and iPhone model.

My session disappeared after I force-quit the app.

It shouldn't have. Holt persists session state to disk at every transition and rehydrates on launch. If you re-open the app and the session is missing, please email hello@holt.app with the approximate time the session started and which mode it was — we'll look at the rehydration logic.

Live Activity isn't showing on my Lock Screen.
  1. iOS Settings → Face ID & Passcode → ensure Lock Screen access for notifications is allowed.
  2. iOS Settings → Holt → Live Activities — toggle on.
  3. End the active session and start a new one. Live Activities are spawned at session start; they don't retroactively attach.
Notifications aren't firing when sessions end.
  1. iOS Settings → Notifications → Holt — confirm Allow Notifications is on.
  2. If you denied the permission prompt on first launch, you'll need to enable it here manually.
  3. Force-quit and reopen Holt to re-register the notification scheduler.
The widget isn't updating.

Holt asks WidgetKit to refresh after every session state change. iOS controls how often widget timelines actually run. If your widget is stuck, remove and re-add it from the Home Screen — that forces a fresh timeline.

I found a bug.

Email hello@holt.app with iOS version, iPhone model, and a description of what you were doing. Screenshots are gold. One person reads everything; expect a reply within a day or two.
