Getting started

First-launch flow and how a session works.

How do I start my first session?
  1. Tap a mode tile near the bottom of the Timer screen: Quick, Pomodoro, or Deep.
  2. Tap the timer disc in the centre, or tap Fen.
  3. The session begins. Any apps you've configured for that mode disappear behind the iOS shield.

If the mode you picked has app blocking configured, iOS will ask for Screen Time permission the first time. Approve to start; decline to run the session as a timer only.

Tap Fen or tap the timer. Is there a difference?

No. Both start the same session. Two tap targets exist because users instinctively reach for the timer or the mascot, and either should work.

Where do notifications come from?

Locally. Holt schedules them through Apple's UserNotifications framework the moment a session starts, so they fire even if the app is backgrounded or force-quit. For chained modes such as Pomodoro, every phase boundary gets its own alert: "Break time" at each work-end, "Back to it" at each break-end, and "Done" when the chain finishes.

What happens if I force-quit Holt during a session?

Nothing bad. Holt writes the session state to disk at every transition. When you reopen the app it picks up where you left off, and the Screen Time shield stays up in the meantime. Force-quitting does not end a session.

App blocking

Screen Time integration and strict mode.

Why does Holt ask for Screen Time access?

Because the actual blocking is done by iOS, not by Holt. The FamilyControls framework powers Apple's own Screen Time feature, and Holt uses it to apply a shield over the apps you flagged. Without that permission Holt can still run a timer, but it cannot block anything.

The framework is designed for privacy: Holt sees opaque tokens, not app names. We cannot read which apps are on your phone.

How do I select apps to block?
  1. Open Settings → Modes in Holt.
  2. Tap the mode you want to configure.
  3. Tap Block apps.
  4. iOS presents its Screen Time picker. Pick apps, categories, or websites and tap Done.

Each mode keeps its own selection. Pomodoro can shield social media while Deep shields everything but your editor.

What's special about Deep mode?

Deep is strict. Once started, the End button is disabled and the shield holds until the timer runs out. Other modes let you end the session early via a confirmation dialog. Use Deep when you genuinely want the option to bail removed.

Can I get around the strict shield?

Not from inside Holt. The shield is enforced at the OS level. You could uninstall the app, but iOS continues to apply Screen Time restrictions briefly after uninstall, and your session record is permanently lost. The polite answer is: don't start a strict session unless you mean it.

Modes

What are the built-in modes?
  • Quick. One 10-minute block, no chain.
  • Pomodoro. 25-minute work blocks, four cycles, 5-minute breaks. 115 minutes wall-clock total.
  • Deep. One 90-minute strict block.
How do I create a custom mode?
  1. On the Timer screen, scroll the modes row to the end and tap + New.
  2. Or open Settings → Modes and tap the + in the section header.
  3. Set the name, duration, cycles, break length, icon, strict toggle, and app selection. Tap Save.

Custom modes appear alongside the built-ins and can be reordered, edited, or deleted from Settings → Modes.

The Pomodoro timer shows 25 minutes. Why not 115?

The on-screen countdown shows the current work block. The chain caption directly below the countdown ("4 cycles · 5m rest · 115m total") shows the full commitment. Notifications fire at every transition so you always know what cycle you're in.

History & data

Where does my session history live?

In Apple's SwiftData store, on your device. There is no Holt server. There is no cloud copy. Uninstalling Holt removes all session history and configuration permanently.

Can I sync across devices?

Not in v1. iCloud sync is on the roadmap; v1 keeps the data-handling surface deliberately small.

How do I export my history?

Export isn't in v1. If you need a way to move history off Holt, email and we'll prioritise the feature based on demand.

How do I delete my data?

Delete Holt from your iPhone. Session records, custom modes, Screen Time selection, and preferences all go with the app. There is no Holt server-side copy.

Does Holt write to Apple Health?

Only if you opt in. Toggle Health on under Settings → Health, and Holt will write completed focus sessions to Apple Health as Mindful Minutes. The write is one-way. Revoke any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Holt.

Billing

What's included in the free tier?

Quick and Pomodoro modes run free, with no time limit and no session cap. The Pro upgrade adds app blocking via Screen Time, the Deep strict mode, custom modes, Apple Health integration, and Siri/Shortcuts.

How are subscriptions billed?

Through your Apple ID. Monthly and annual subscriptions auto-renew unless cancelled at least 24 hours before the period ends. The annual plan includes a 7-day free trial. Lifetime is a one-time purchase with no renewal.

How do I cancel?
  1. iOS Settings → tap your name at the top.
  2. Subscriptions → Holt → Cancel Subscription.

Holt does not manage subscription state directly; Apple handles all of it.

I bought lifetime. Will it transfer to a new iPhone?

Yes. On the new device, open the paywall and tap Restore Purchases. StoreKit detects your prior purchase under the same Apple ID and unlocks the app.

How do I request a refund?

Apple manages all refunds. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com, find the Holt charge, and request a refund there. We have no access to your payment information and cannot process refunds directly.

Troubleshooting

The shield isn't blocking an app I selected.
  1. Confirm Screen Time permission. iOS Settings → Holt → Screen Time should show Allow Family Controls on.
  2. End the current session and start a new one. The shield is applied at session start.
  3. Toggle iOS Screen Time off and back on (iOS Settings → Screen Time).
  4. Restart the iPhone. Screen Time wedges occasionally; a restart clears it.

If the issue persists, email us with your iOS version and iPhone model.

Live Activity isn't showing on my Lock Screen.
  1. iOS Settings → Face ID & Passcode → ensure Lock Screen access for notifications is allowed.
  2. iOS Settings → Holt → Live Activities. Toggle on.
  3. End the active session and start a new one. Live Activities are spawned at session start and don't retroactively attach.
I'm not getting notifications when sessions end.
  1. iOS Settings → Notifications → Holt. Confirm Allow Notifications is on.
  2. If you denied the prompt on first launch, you'll need to enable it here manually.
  3. Force-quit and reopen Holt to re-register the notification scheduler.
The widget isn't updating.

Holt asks WidgetKit to refresh after every session state change, but iOS controls how often widget timelines actually run. If your widget is stuck, remove and re-add it from the Home Screen. That forces a fresh timeline.

Found a bug.

Email hello@holt.app with iOS version, iPhone model, and a description of what you were doing when it broke. Screenshots are gold. One person reads everything; expect a reply within a day or two.
