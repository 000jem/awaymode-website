Getting started

How do I start a session?

Open Holt. Pick a mode from the row at the bottom — Quick, Pomodoro, or Deep. Tap the timer or tap Fen. The session begins.

What's the difference between the modes?

Quick is one 10-minute sit. Good for the in-between moments.
Pomodoro is the classic 25/5 chain, four times through — about two hours total with breaks.
Deep is 90 minutes of strict focus. The latch stays shut. You can also tap the New tile to build your own.

Why does Holt ask for Screen Time permission?

Holt uses Apple's Screen Time framework (also called Family Controls) to actually block the apps you choose. Without that permission, Holt can still run a timer, but it can't shut anything out. Screen Time is the Apple-provided way to block apps without compromising your privacy — Holt never sees the names of the apps you pick.

Blocking and modes

How do I pick which apps to block?

Open Settings → Modes, tap the mode you want to edit, then tap Block apps. iOS will show its Screen Time picker — pick apps, categories, or websites. Tap Done. The selection saves to that mode.

What does Deep Holt do that the other modes don't?

Deep Holt is the strict mode. Once you start a session in it, you can't cancel mid-session by tapping End. The shield holds until the timer runs out. It's there for the days you really need to be unable to change your mind.

The shield isn't blocking the app I picked.

First, make sure you've granted Screen Time access in iOS Settings → Holt → Screen Time. If access is granted but the block isn't working, try:

  • End the session and start a new one (re-applies the shield).
  • Toggle Screen Time off and back on in iOS Settings.
  • Restart your phone. (Genuinely — Screen Time sometimes wedges itself.)

Still stuck? Email hello@holt.app with the iOS version and the iPhone model. Screen Time has a few quirks we can usually narrow down quickly.

History, Stats, and data

Where does my session history live?

On your phone. Holt uses Apple's SwiftData to store everything locally. There is no Holt server. Uninstalling the app removes the history.

Can I sync history across devices?

Not in v1. iCloud sync is on the roadmap, but Holt v1 is single-device on purpose — fewer moving parts, fewer ways for your data to leak.

How do I delete my data?

Uninstall Holt. Every session record, mode, and preference is removed with the app. There's nothing on a server to wipe because there is no server.

Does Holt send any data to Apple Health?

Only if you opt in. Holt can write completed focus sessions to Apple Health as Mindful Minutes. It's one-way: Holt writes, Holt does not read. You can revoke this any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health.

Subscription and pricing

What's free vs paid?

The Quick and Pomodoro modes are free, and the timer runs whether or not you pay. App blocking and the Deep mode are part of the paid tier. The paywall appears the first time you try to start a paid feature.

How do I cancel a subscription?

iOS Settings → tap your name at the top → Subscriptions → Holt → Cancel. Apple handles all subscription state; we never see your billing details.

I bought lifetime on one device. Will it work on a new one?

Yes. Tap Restore Purchases on the paywall in Holt on the new device — Apple's StoreKit will recognise your purchase and unlock the app.

I want a refund.

Apple handles all refunds. Go to reportaproblem.apple.com, find the Holt purchase, and request a refund. We don't have access to your payment information and can't process refunds ourselves.

Why a fennec fox?

A "holt" is the den of a fox. Fen is a fennec — small, alert, quiet, in his prime. He fits the brand: a sentinel, not a coach.

Will Holt come to iPad or Mac?

Maybe. v1 is iPhone-only on purpose. If enough people ask for iPad, it's not a huge port. Mac is further out.
